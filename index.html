What Is a Delivery Docket?

A delivery docket is proof that materials/services were delivered to site. It provides traceability ensuring materials charged to the project were actually delivered.

The Three-Way Verification

  • Purchase Order — what was ordered
  • Delivery Docket — what was delivered
  • Supplier Invoice — what is being charged

Financial Control Rule: No Docket + No PO = Invoice NOT Payable. Invoice will be rejected and payment withheld.

Docket Must Confirm

  • Supplier name and date
  • Materials delivered and quantities
  • Purchase Order (PO) reference
  • Signed by authorised site personnel
  • Filed / uploaded into Plexa

What Is a Site Diary?

The Site Diary is a legal and operational record of daily activities. Completed daily by the Site Manager, Foreman, or Site Engineer and recorded digitally through Plexa.

Recorded in Plexa

  • Weather conditions
  • Workforce numbers (man count)
  • Trades and subcontractors on site
  • Deliveries and material movements
  • Plant and equipment in operation
  • Delays or disruptions
  • Safety incidents or hazards
  • Inspections and site visits

Why Site Diaries Matter

  • Evidence in contractual disputes
  • Delay analysis and extension of time claims
  • Workforce attendance trends
  • Productivity tracking

What Is an ITP?

An Inspection Test Plan (ITP) is a quality control document used on construction projects to ensure works are inspected and verified at critical stages of construction.

ITPs Define

  • What must be inspected
  • When inspections occur
  • Hold points / witness points
  • Compliance with NCC / AS / BCA

Inspection Types

  • Hold Point: Work must stop until inspection approval is granted
  • Witness Point: Inspector may witness but work may proceed if they don't attend
  • Verification: Internal quality checks by the project team

Work Requiring ITP Inspections

  • Structural reinforcement prior to concrete pours
  • Waterproofing installation
  • Fire penetration sealing
  • Services installations
  • Finishes and façade elements

What Are Construction Site Turnstiles?

Turnstiles are controlled access gates at site entry points. They ensure only authorised and inducted personnel can enter. Integrated with Plexa to manage worker inductions and access credentials.

How the System Works

  • Workers complete a site induction in Plexa
  • Credentials (QR code / card / mobile) registered in Plexa
  • Turnstile scans credentials before allowing entry
  • Entry recorded for attendance and safety tracking

No Induction = No Entry. All workers must complete site induction before arriving on site.

Why Controlled Access Matters

  • WHS Act & Regulations (NSW) compliance
  • Emergency evacuation and headcount requirements
  • Prevents uninducted workers on site
  • Maintains accurate workforce records

What Is Attendance Tracking?

Records the number of workers on site, their trade, and working hours. Provides data to monitor labour resources and ensure works progress per the construction program.

Data Captured Through

  • Turnstile access systems connected to Plexa
  • Daily Site Diary man counts recorded in Plexa

Insights Cadets Can Analyse

  • Sudden drop in manpower → potential delay
  • Trade overpopulation → congestion risk
  • Consistent low numbers → program risk

Attendance Supports

  • Safety compliance and emergency headcounts
  • Labour verification and cost control
  • Productivity analysis
  • Program delivery monitoring

Eligibility

Academic Enrollment
& Requirements

During your time in the program, you are expected to be enrolled in, or have completed, approved formal building-related studies.

At the start of each semester, you'll need to provide a copy of your University timetable to both your immediate supervisor and HR for approval.

In most cases, cadets will be automatically approved to attend university for one day per week. If you require more than one day off, special consideration is needed.

The company may offer special leave with full pay for studying and attending exams.

During semester breaks, you are expected to attend work on days that would normally be university days.

At the end of each semester, you'll be asked to send your university results to HR. Results remain confidential between your supervisor, senior management, and HR.

On your Graduation Day, you will be given a day off to attend the ceremony — provided you have completed a minimum of 12 months of continuous service.

Builtcom understands that balancing work and study can be challenging. If you ever find it difficult to manage your responsibilities, reach out for support before it becomes a bigger issue.

Safety First

Builtcom
Big 9 Checklist

Builtcom Safe is more than a slogan — it's who we are. Safety is embedded in our culture and guides everything we do.

1

Penetrations & Voids

All penetrations and voids must be sealed, covered, or controlled to prevent falls or dropped objects.

  • Cover and seal penetrations immediately after works
  • Barricade if penetrations can't be closed straight away
  • Never remove another trade's cover without approval
2

Working Platforms & Scaffold

Scaffold and working platforms must be stable, compliant, and clear of obstructions at all times.

  • Only licensed scaffolders may adjust scaffold
  • Keep platforms clear of rubbish, tools, or materials
  • Do not use scaffold tagged "incomplete" or "unsafe"
  • No domestic ladders allowed on site
3

Edge Protection & Work at Height

Every live edge and opening must have proper fall-prevention controls in place.

  • Never remove or alter edge protection barriers
  • Report any missing or unsafe barriers immediately
  • Treat all edges as live until confirmed safe
4

Traffic Management

Pedestrian and vehicle movements must always follow the approved traffic plan.

  • Use only designated walkways and access points
  • Follow instructions from spotters, signage, and traffic controllers
  • Do not adjust barriers or signage without approval
5

Housekeeping & Storage

A clean, orderly site prevents accidents and improves productivity.

  • Clean up your work area daily
  • Keep storage zones tidy, labelled, and organised
  • Wear correct PPE and manage silica dust properly
6

Access, Egress & Exclusion Zones

Safe entry, exit, and exclusion areas must always be maintained and respected.

  • Keep emergency exits and access routes clear
  • Respect fencing, hoardings, and exclusion zones at all times
  • Cap or remove protruding objects that could cause injury

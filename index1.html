The Cadet Program

How the Program
Works

The Builtcom Cadet Program combines formal education with structured hands-on learning in the workplace. Unlike most programs, we won't box you into set rotations across every division. While the program runs for six months, your time here should feel meaningful — not just ticked off a checklist.

If you find your passion in one area, we'll encourage you to go deep. Every six months, you'll sit down with your manager for a dedicated review — a chance to celebrate milestones and map out your goals ahead.

Eligibility

Academic Enrollment
& Requirements

During your time in the program, you are expected to be enrolled in, or have completed, approved formal building-related studies.

At the start of each semester, you'll need to provide a copy of your University timetable to both your immediate supervisor and HR for approval.

In most cases, cadets will be automatically approved to attend university for one day per week. If you require more than one day off, special consideration is needed.

The company may offer special leave with full pay for studying and attending exams.

During semester breaks, you are expected to attend work on days that would normally be university days.

At the end of each semester, you'll be asked to send your university results to HR. Results remain confidential between your supervisor, senior management, and HR.

On your Graduation Day, you will be given a day off to attend the ceremony — provided you have completed a minimum of 12 months of continuous service.

Builtcom understands that balancing work and study can be challenging. If you ever find it difficult to manage your responsibilities, reach out for support before it becomes a bigger issue.

Safety First

Builtcom
Big 9 Checklist

Builtcom Safe is more than a slogan — it's who we are. Safety is embedded in our culture and guides everything we do.

1

Penetrations & Voids

All penetrations and voids must be sealed, covered, or controlled to prevent falls or dropped objects.

  • Cover and seal penetrations immediately after works
  • Barricade if penetrations can't be closed straight away
  • Never remove another trade's cover without approval
2

Working Platforms & Scaffold

Scaffold and working platforms must be stable, compliant, and clear of obstructions at all times.

  • Only licensed scaffolders may adjust scaffold
  • Keep platforms clear of rubbish, tools, or materials
  • Do not use scaffold tagged "incomplete" or "unsafe"
  • No domestic ladders allowed on site
3

Edge Protection & Work at Height

Every live edge and opening must have proper fall-prevention controls in place.

  • Never remove or alter edge protection barriers
  • Report any missing or unsafe barriers immediately
  • Treat all edges as live until confirmed safe
4

Traffic Management

Pedestrian and vehicle movements must always follow the approved traffic plan.

  • Use only designated walkways and access points
  • Follow instructions from spotters, signage, and traffic controllers
  • Do not adjust barriers or signage without approval
5

Housekeeping & Storage

A clean, orderly site prevents accidents and improves productivity.

  • Clean up your work area daily
  • Keep storage zones tidy, labelled, and organised
  • Wear correct PPE and manage silica dust properly
6

Access, Egress & Exclusion Zones

Safe entry, exit, and exclusion areas must always be maintained and respected.

  • Keep emergency exits and access routes clear
  • Respect fencing, hoardings, and exclusion zones at all times
  • Cap or remove protruding objects that could cause injury
